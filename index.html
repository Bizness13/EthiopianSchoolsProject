What we do Donate
Seattle-based nonprofit supporting education in Ethiopia

We support elementary students in rural Ethiopia by building schools and providing educational resources so that every child can learn to read, write, and achieve elementary school proficiency.

Founded by Surafel (Sasu) Techane through TeffLabs, the Ethiopian Schools Project channels proceeds from TeffBar sales and community donations to construct schools and deliver learning materials in underserved Ethiopian communities. We partner with local educators and families to create sustainable educational opportunities focused on literacy and foundational learning.

Legacy: Founded in 2002, our earliest major effort supported the renovation and expansion of Armania Elementary & Junior Secondary School in Armania Kebele, Tarmaber Woreda (North Shoa), Ethiopia.

Our program areas

We focus on literacy and foundational learning by supporting rural Ethiopian schools with materials, infrastructure, and student support.

We provide textbooks, writing materials, educational posters, and learning supplies to schools in rural Ethiopia where resources are scarce. Materials are selected based on Ethiopian curriculum requirements for grades 1-8, with emphasis on Amharic and English literacy development.

üè´ School improvements

Classrooms, libraries, and infrastructure

We fund construction of new classrooms, library spaces, and essential infrastructure including clean water access, sanitation facilities, and solar power for lighting. Each project is developed in partnership with local communities to ensure long-term sustainability and maintenance.

üéí Student support

Removing barriers to learning

We remove barriers to education by providing school uniforms, covering enrollment fees, offering tutoring programs in reading and writing, and supporting meal programs to ensure students can focus on learning.
