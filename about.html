About Ethiopian Schools Project

We support elementary students in rural Ethiopia by building schools and providing educational resources so that every child can learn to read, write, and achieve elementary school proficiency.

Vision

A future where every child in rural Ethiopia has access to quality elementary education in safe, well-equipped schools with trained teachers and abundant learning materials.

Values

  • Community Partnership: We work alongside Ethiopian educators, families, and local leaders
  • Educational Equity: Every child deserves access to literacy and foundational learning
  • Sustainability: We build lasting infrastructure and local capacity
  • Transparency: We openly share our impact and financial stewardship
  • Innovation: From TeffBar to school construction, we find creative ways to fund education

Partnership approach

We collaborate directly with Ethiopian communities, local education officials, and families to identify needs, design projects, and ensure schools are maintained and supported long-term. Our approach respects local knowledge and builds on existing community strengths.

Founder’s story

The Ethiopian Schools Project was founded by Surafel (Sasu) Techane, who created TeffLabs and the TeffBar as an innovative way to fund educational opportunities in Ethiopia. Sasu's vision was simple but powerful: every child deserves to read, write, and learn. Today, under the leadership of Zebene Techane and Abby Mulugeta, we continue Sasu's legacy by building schools and supporting literacy across rural Ethiopia.
